## Summary

The expert sets out how Danish vehicle-tax flows — customs inspection (*Toldsyn*), registration tax (*registreringsafgift*), the licence-plate outlay, and the export refund of registration tax (*Eksportgodtgørelse*) — should be modelled in the Vehicle Trade module. The concrete proposal is **four new JobTypes** (with three matching SKUs) so each cost or refund posts to its own e-conomic account, **a specific formula adjustment** for margin-scheme VAT when an EU export refund is involved, and **a UI warning** when invoicing a non-margin-scheme vehicle. The end goal is a complete per-vehicle expense/income report from the JobCard history.

## Points

1. **Danish vehicle taxes are uniquely high — up to 200–300 % on a new vehicle.** *Registreringsafgift* (registration tax) is therefore a material cost on import and a material refundable amount on export. It must be modelled explicitly in the Vehicle Trade module.

2. **Every e-conomic transaction posted by the Vehicle Trade module must carry the unique vehicle number** so each cost and income line can be tied to a single vehicle in downstream reporting.

3. **Add four new JobTypes** to the two already in use (*Reparationsarbejdstype* — repair; *Garantiarbejdstype* — warranty):
   1. **Toldsyn** — customs inspection (on import).
   2. **Udlæg for nummerplader** — outlay (pass-through cost) for licence plates.
   3. **Udlæg for registreringsafgift** — outlay for the registration tax.
   4. **Eksportgodtgørelse** — export refund of the registration tax.

4. **Add three matching SKUs** — one each for the first three JobTypes (Toldsyn, *Udlæg for nummerplader*, *Udlæg for registreringsafgift*). No SKU is needed for *Eksportgodtgørelse* because it is a refund, not a sales line. Each SKU is to carry a *Product Segment* mapped to a specific e-conomic account.

5. **Vehicle AddOn configuration must extend to the four new JobTypes.** Each must be:
   - selectable in the JobType dropdown alongside repair/warranty, and
   - configured with an e-conomic account number (*Toldsynkonto*, *Nummerpladekonto*, *Registreringsafgiftkonto*, *Eksportgodtgørelseskonto*) so that when the JobCard is closed, the cost/income posts to the correct ledger account.

6. **VAT treatment of the three new SKUs on sale:**

   | SKU                            | VAT on resale | Reason given                  |
   | ------------------------------ | ------------- | ----------------------------- |
   | Toldsyn                        | **WITH** VAT  | Service                       |
   | Udlæg for nummerplader         | **WITHOUT** VAT | Pass-through outlay         |
   | Udlæg for registreringsafgift  | **WITHOUT** VAT | Pass-through outlay         |

7. **The *Registreringsafgift* JobCard is bidirectional.** It records the registration tax in both directions — positive on sale (charged to the buyer) and negative on purchase (paid out / withheld). The same JobType must support both signs.

8. **Eksportgodtgørelse (export refund) workflow.** When the workshop exports a vehicle out of Denmark, it may apply to the state for a refund of the original *registreringsafgift*. Key properties:
   - The refund **can take months** to be confirmed.
   - In the meantime, the user records an **estimated** value on an *Eksportgodtgørelse* JobCard. The Told & Skat online calculator is used to produce that estimate: <https://motorregister.skat.dk/dmr-kerne/registreringsafgift/beregnRegistreringsafgift?execution=e1s1>
   - When the actual refund arrives, the user updates the JobCard and closes it; the corrected amount then posts to e-conomic.
   - **Feature request:** link directly to the Told & Skat calculator from inside the Vehicle Trade module.

9. **Margin-scheme VAT calculation, with export-refund adjustment.**
   - Standard margin-scheme VAT formula (Diff moms):
     `Diff moms = (Sale × 25 / 125) − (Cost × 25 / 125)`
   - When selling/exporting a margin-scheme vehicle **inside the EU**, the *Eksportgodtgørelse* value is deducted from the sale price before the margin calculation:
     `Diff moms = ((Sale − Refund) × 25 / 125) − (Cost × 25 / 125)`
   - In most realistic cases this produces a **negative** number. A negative result must be **transferred to e-conomic as zero** — margin VAT is floored at zero, never refunded.
   - Worked example provided by the expert:

     | Item                                       | Amount (DKK) |
     | ------------------------------------------ | -----------: |
     | Cost — bought from a Danish private person |       50 000 |
     | Expected *Eksportgodtgørelse* refund       |       20 000 |
     | Sale price to an EU private or company     |       55 000 |
     | `((55 000 − 20 000) × 25 / 125) − (50 000 × 25 / 125)` = `7 000 − 10 000` | **−3 000 → posted as 0** |

10. **Warning when invoicing a non-margin-scheme vehicle.** If the vehicle was **not** bought and sold under the margin scheme, the user should see a warning at sales-invoice time:
    > *"Remember to add registreringsafgift on invoice if required."*

    The point is to nudge the user to add the registration-tax line on full-VAT sales where it is not implicitly carried in the inventory value.

11. **End goal: a complete per-vehicle expense/income view.** With the new JobTypes and SKUs in place, the system can show every cost and every income line for a single vehicle across its trade life — report and overview. The level of granularity is configurable per workshop.

## Ambiguities

- **Author identity is not stated.** `TS1414` looks like an internal document or ticket reference. Worth confirming who wrote this.
- **Four JobTypes vs three SKUs.** The text introduces four new JobTypes but only three SKUs. I read *Eksportgodtgørelse* as JobType-only (because it is a refund rather than a saleable line), but this should be confirmed.
- **EU vs non-EU export refund.** The formula adjustment in point 9 is described **inside the EU**. The document does not explicitly state whether the same *Eksportgodtgørelse* deduction applies to **non-EU** exports — logically it should, but unconfirmed.
- **Where is the floor-at-zero applied — line level or document level?** A multi-line invoice with mixed positive and negative margin contributions is not discussed.
- **"JobType" vs "Job Card"**. The expert speaks of JobTypes as the unit; current `docs/` uses *Job Card* for the record of work and is silent on a JobType taxonomy. Treating *JobType* as a category attribute of a Job Card is a reasonable mapping but should be confirmed.
- **"Vehicle AddOn" and "Product Segment"** are platform concepts not yet described in `docs/`. The notes treat them as given and defer their introduction to the analysis step.
- **e-conomic is named as the target accounting system.** Current `docs/` keeps the accounting integration abstract; whether e-conomic is *the* target or one of several is unclear from this document alone.
